# IBCS Alumni Market Research
## Research Summary: IBCS Certified Professionals as Target Market

**Research Date:** December 19, 2025
**Prepared by:** Mary, Business Analyst
**Purpose:** Validate IBCS certification alumni as a target market for TellingCube, based on feedback from Jürgen Faisst

---

## Executive Summary

The IBCS (International Business Communication Standards) certification alumni market represents a significant and well-defined target audience for TellingCube. With **12,384+ IBCS Association members** from **139 countries** and **6,000+ training participants** from approximately 1,000 companies, this community demonstrates strong engagement with standardized business reporting practices.

**Key Finding:** IBCS practitioners face a critical implementation challenge - while they understand reporting standards, they struggle with the 3-4 year timeline to implement IBCS across organizations and face resistance to change. TellingCube can position itself as the tool that makes IBCS adoption faster and easier.

## Research Findings

### 1. Market Size: IBCS Certifications Issued Globally

**IBCS Association Membership (as of June 2024):**
- **12,384 members** from **139 countries**
- Free membership available at two levels:
  - **Passive members:** Express interest in IBCS standards
  - **Active members:** In-depth knowledge of standards, voting rights (typically IBCS Certified Consultants and experienced customers)

**Training Participants:**
- **6,000+ participants** from approximately **1,000 companies** of all sizes across all sectors
- Training delivered publicly and in-house
- Companies range from Fortune 100 firms to mid-sized enterprises

**Certification Levels:**
1. **IBCS Certified Analyst** - Targets individuals who apply IBCS standards to reports, presentations, and dashboards
2. **IBCS Certified Consultant** - Project managers with years of experience in Controlling and/or Business Intelligence
   - Required to complete Certified Analyst first
   - 3-day consultant training program
   - Annual recertification required
   - Fee: €3,500 per person

**Note:** Specific breakdown of certified analysts vs. consultants not publicly available.

### 3. Roles IBCS Alumni Typically Hold

**Primary Professional Roles:**
1. **Controllers/Controlling Professionals**
   - Certified Controller designation exists within IBCS framework
   - Top management (CEO, CFO) involvement critical for successful implementation

2. **CFOs and Finance Leaders**
   - IBCS emphasizes that standardizing report design "frees space for strategic decisions for CFOs"
   - CFO buy-in essential for organization-wide adoption

3. **Business Intelligence (BI) Analysts**
   - Certified Business Intelligence Professional (CBIP) designation available
   - Focus on Leadership & Management applications

4. **Business Analysts**
   - Specific consultant type category in IBCS directory
   - Apply standards to reports, presentations, and dashboards

5. **FP&A Professionals**
   - IBCS described as "an initiative of the FP&A and Business Intelligence community"

6. **Consultants and Project Managers**
   - Internal project managers implementing IBCS in organizations
   - External consultants helping companies adopt standards
   - Many years of experience in Controlling and/or BI required

**IBCS Certified Providers:**
- Organizations with 10%+ employees as IBCS Certified Consultants
- Minimum 3 IBCS Certified Consultants
- Must present at least 3 IBCS-compliant sample solutions

## Key Pain Points and Opportunities

### Implementation Challenges IBCS Practitioners Face:

1. **Long Implementation Timeline**
   - Global rollouts take **3-4 years**
   - "Not a weekend job"

2. **Management Resistance**
   - "Toughest challenge" is getting management buy-in
   - People reluctant to change concepts they're proud of
   - Don't want to follow "external rules"

3. **Perceived Inflexibility**
   - Standards seen as too rigid by some companies
   - Obstacle in fast-moving industries requiring quick decisions

4. **Technical Limitations**
   - IBCS "Unify" rule covers one version per scenario (Actual, Plan, Forecast)
   - Companies often need multiple versions (e.g., FC1-FC4)
   - Charts struggle to differentiate between versions visually

5. **Inconsistent Reports**
   - Organizations suffer from different-looking reports
   - Design follows personal taste of creators
   - Hard to understand across teams

### Proven Benefits (When Successfully Implemented):

- **46% faster information processing** (Technical University of Munich study)
- **61% reduction in interpretation errors**
- Standardized communication across organizations
- Better decision-making for management teams
