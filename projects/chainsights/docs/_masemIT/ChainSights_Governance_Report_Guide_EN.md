## The 5 Core Questions Every Report Should Answer

Before diving into details, check if the report answers these fundamental questions:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| **Who is voting?** | A few whales or a broad community? |
| **How concentrated is power?** | Do 10 wallets control 90% of votes? |
| **Is there real participation?** | Or is it "governance theater"? |
| **What's the trend?** | Getting better or worse? |
| **What should we change?** | Concrete recommendations |

If any of these answers are missing, the report is incomplete.

## Key Metrics Explained

### 1. Voter Participation Rate

```
Formula: Unique Voters / Total Token Holders √ó 100
```

**What it means:**
- What percentage of eligible voters actually voted?

**How to read it:**

| Value | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| >30% | Excellent ‚Äî active, engaged community |
| 10-30% | Normal ‚Äî typical for DAOs |
| 5-10% | Low ‚Äî apathy or information gap |
| <5% | Critical ‚Äî "governance theater" risk |

**But beware:** A high rate isn't automatically good if only whales are voting!

### 2. Voting Power Concentration

```
Typical format: "Top 10 wallets control X% of voting power"
```

**The truth behind the numbers:**

| Metric | Healthy | Critical |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Top 1 Wallet | <10% | >25% |
| Top 10 Wallets | <40% | >60% |
| Top 100 Wallets | <70% | >90% |

**Critical distinction:**
- **Whale Concentration** = Large token holders
- **Delegate Concentration** = Large amounts of delegated voting power

A delegate with 20% voting power is different from a whale with 20% ‚Äî the delegate was chosen by the community!

### 3. Gini Coefficient

```
Scale: 0 to 1
0 = Perfectly equal distribution
1 = One person owns everything
```

**For DAOs:**

| Value | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| <0.5 | Highly decentralized ‚Äî unusually good |
| 0.5-0.7 | Decentralized ‚Äî good distribution |
| 0.7-0.85 | Concentrated ‚Äî typical for DAOs |
| >0.85 | Highly concentrated ‚Äî oligarchy risk |

**Context matters:** A Gini of 0.8 can be acceptable if top holders are active delegates who operate transparently.

### 4. Unique Voters vs. Voting Power

```
Example:
- 1,000 unique voters ‚úì
- Top 5 voters hold 80% of voting power ‚ö†Ô∏è
```

**What this means:**
Many people are voting (good!), but few are deciding (problematic).

**How to read it:**
- Don't just count wallets ‚Äî weigh their power
- Ideal: Broad participation AND broad power distribution

### 5. Proposal Success Rate

```
Formula: Passed Proposals / All Proposals √ó 100
```

**Interpretation:**

| Value | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| >95% | Too high ‚Äî are all proposals rubber-stamped? |
| 70-90% | Healthy ‚Äî filter works but isn't blocking |
| 50-70% | Contested ‚Äî active debate |
| <50% | Problematic ‚Äî governance is gridlocked |

**Important:** A 100% success rate is not good news ‚Äî it means either nobody disagrees, or controversial proposals aren't being submitted at all.

## Red Flags: When Should You Be Concerned?

### üö© Immediate Red Flags

| Flag | What You See | What It Means |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| **Ghost Governance** | 0 proposals in 3+ months | DAO is inactive or dead |
| **Rubber Stamp** | 100% approval, <5% turnout | Fake decentralization |
| **Whale Dominance** | 1 wallet >30% voting power | One person controls everything |
| **Trend Collapse** | Participation -50% vs. prior quarter | Community losing interest |

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs (not critical, but monitor)

| Signal | What You See | Recommendation |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Declining participation | -10-20% vs. prior period | Improve communication |
| Rising concentration | +5% for Top 10 | Promote delegation |
| Few proposals | <2/month for large DAO | Simplify governance process |
| High abstentions | >20% "Abstain" votes | Write clearer proposals |

## How to Use Report Insights Practically

### For Community Calls

**Prepare 3 key statements:**

1. **The Headline Stat:** "Our participation increased by 15%!"
2. **The Honest Look:** "But 70% of voting power is held by 20 wallets."
3. **The Call-to-Action:** "We need to strengthen delegation."

### For Improvement Proposals

**Use data as your argument:**

> "According to our governance report, 47% of proposals had less than 10% participation. I propose we introduce a minimum quorum of 5% to strengthen legitimacy."

### For Investor Relations

**Show maturity through transparency:**

> "Our Governance Health Metrics show a Gini of 0.72 ‚Äî concentrated, but comparable to leading DAOs. We're actively working on decentralization through our Delegate Program."

## Questions to Ask Your Report Provider

When commissioning or receiving a governance report, ask these questions:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|

| "How current is it?" | Relevance |
| "Do you distinguish whales from delegates?" | Analytical depth |
| "Is there a benchmark?" | Context |
| "What do you specifically recommend?" | Actionability |
